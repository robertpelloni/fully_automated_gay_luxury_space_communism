# /office-hours — Product Brainstorming & Validation

Adapted from [Garry Tan's gstack](https://github.com/garrytan/gstack) `/office-hours` skill.

## When to Use

Run `/office-hours` when starting something new. Before writing any code, this workflow reframes your idea through the lens of a YC office hours session.

## How It Works

1. **Detect mode** — Is this a startup/business idea (Startup Mode) or a personal/creative project (Builder Mode)?

2. **Ask the Six Forcing Questions** (Startup Mode):
   - What is the **specific pain** you've experienced? (Not hypothetical — real, lived pain)
   - Who **exactly** are the first 10 users? (Name them or describe them specifically)
   - What do they do **today** to solve this? (The current workaround IS your competition)
   - Why will **your** solution be 10x better? (Not 2x — 10x or don't bother)
   - What's the **narrowest possible wedge**? (Ship in days, not months)
   - How will you **know it's working**? (Specific metric, specific number, specific timeframe)

3. **Challenge premises** — Push back on the user's framing. "You said X, but what you actually described is Y."

4. **Generate approaches** — Produce 2-3 implementation approaches with effort estimates.

5. **Write a design document** — Create a structured design doc that feeds into `/plan-ceo-review` and `/plan-eng-review`.

## Builder Mode (Non-Startup Projects)

For creative or personal projects, skip the business validation and focus on:
- What's the **core experience** you want to create?
- What's the **simplest version** that proves the concept?
- What are the **technical risks** worth spiking first?

## Key Principles

- Listen to the **pain**, not the **feature request**
- The user often describes a feature when they mean a product
- Always recommend the narrowest wedge that validates the core hypothesis
- Show effort estimates in both human and AI-assisted time

## Output

A design document saved to the project that downstream workflows can read and build on.
